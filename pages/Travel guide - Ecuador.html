In chronological order, I go through the places I have been this trip. I will start each location with some general tips and go further into the plans I made, hostels and restaurants.
Additionaly I made a list in maps containing all the spots I got tipped about or found to be really nice

Quito
Good to know before:
  1. The hostels I mainly book through the app "Hostelworld"
  1. A save and (relatively)cheap way of getting around in cities is by using the apps "InDrive" and Uber.  This mainly works in the big cities, outside maybe less.
  1. There are lots of busses, the ones between the cities are generally quite safe. Maybe local busses within the cities tend to get less safe as they are fuller.
  1. Sunscreen is really expensive so if possible bring it yourself
  1. Through most hostels, they offer a pick-ups service by taxi from the airport. I found this ideal after the flight.
  1. Using a fanny pack is recommended
Experiences in Quito: [3 full days]
  • The free walking tour offered by "the Secret Garden" hostel at 10am or by "the Community Hostel" at 10:30 am [you can also join these while staying in a different hostel since they are free and work based upon tipping the guide afterwards]
  • Going up in the "Basilica"; you can go up in all 3 towers. Definitely go into "El Torre del Condor" aswell as the left clock-tower. The last bar up in the tower, right under the clocks, allows you to drink a craft beer in the chirch while enjoying the view of Quito.
  • Go to "Mercado Central" and have lunch[suggestion: "Encabollado", this is the local fish soup with onions] on the upper floor.
  • Go up "El Panecillo" [the hill with the winged Virgin statue] around 18u pm. This way you can see the sunset and after see the city light up. But take a taxi up and down, the neighborhoods on "El Panecillo" are dangerous to walk through. Keep in mind that there will be less taxis arriving when you would want to go down. You might want to ask the taxi driver for a retour and to stay with you, this will ofc cost more and might cut the time you can stay up and make the taxi driver wait. The sunset is around 18:30.
  • Hike up a or take up the cable cart to "El Teleferico", if time allows you can walk through to "El Pichincha" [I couldn't do this because the area of "El Teleferico" was closed] Be careful with the altitude sickness if you take the cable cart
  • Hike through "Reserva Geobotanica Pululahua". There will be a bus to Mindo from the "La Ofelia" [the North busstation]. Make sure to know where you need to exit because the bus might not stop there l, so you have to ask. Best would be to go where you can walk to "Museo Templo del Sol". For the way back you'll have to hitch-hike a bus along the road to "La Ofelia"
  • Visit "El Mitad del Mundo", the equator. I would visit a different place than we went to[Museo de Sito Intinan]. You can go to the big place with museums or to Cayambe where the experience might be better.
Places to stay:
  • The Secret Garden Hostel
  • The Community Hostel
These are two social hostels. The Secret Garden
Hostel offers a lot of tours and activities aswell.
"Must-know's"
  1. North bus station: "La Ofelia"
[from here you can take busses to locations north of Quito]
  1. South Bus Station: "Quitombe"
[from here busses go in all kinds of directions]
  1. For a bit of orientation:
When standing in the historical center of Quito. Looking at the Basilica is in the northern direction, looking at El Panecillo is looking in the southern direction.

Mindo:
Good to know before:
  1. Busses to Mindo go 4 times a day; from "La Ofelia" in Quito they go at 8am, 9am, 11am and 16pm.
From Mindo to Quito it differs slightly; the time I don't know exactly but you can ask the hostel. There is one at 6am, 15:15pm and 17pm I believe. But there should be one at 11am aswell  I think.
  1. There is no ATM in Mindo that accepts international cards, so going there you should have enough cash. Otherwise it will be a 1 retour trip by taxi to get cash in "San Miguel de los Bancos"
  1. Insect repellent is a good thing to bring
Experiences in Mindo: [1 full day]
  • "Cascadas de Nambillo" are beautiful. After a hike/taxi drive up you get to a cable cart going over the valley for $5, after the cable cart there are multiple routes to hike. It's beautiful as you hike through the cloud forrest and visit waterfalls. You can take a dip in the first one of the route containing 5 waterfalls.
  • The Yumbos chocolate tour really shows the whole proces of making chocolate and allows you to taste plenty. It's nice and interesting to see
  • Bird watching; in the end I don't do this but Mindo is the place to do it. A good place to do it is "Hacienda San Vincente / Yellow House Trail & Lodge" l. Go early in the morning, they say around 6am-11am is the best. For last-minute booking I got the following contact: Sandra Patino, +593 96 799 6256. She is the birdguide there and best contacted through calling through whatssapp.
Places to stay:
I stayed at the "Cinnamon House" and would recommend. It's a more relaxed and calm place but a good place to meet different travellers aswell. You can cook yourself.
"Must-know's
  • San Miguel de los Bancos; nearest town for getting cash
  • Restaurant near Yumbos [see maps list] cheap lunch but not vegetarian. You'll get a soup and meal for $6
  • Bus station [see maps list], no 100% guarantee on the location but walk along the main road of Mindo: Av. Quito, past the park and at some point you'll find the bus station.

Jungle lodge:
Good to know before:
  1. Vaccinations are key for this region [the place we went to was safe in the end but not worth gambling with I would say]
  1. The weather can switch a couple of times a day so be prepared for that
  1. Going with a guide is essential and very much worth it from a learning point of view, and safety ofc, but also accessibility. [Without a guide wasnt even possible I think for us]
  1. Take a good look at how you'll get to the place you're going, for us it wwas 2 busses and a taxi,could also be 4 busses. The place you're going to likely has info on that
Experiences: [1 full day]
  1. Watch a local family make chocolate and get to know the chocolate making proces
  1. Visit an animal rescue center "AmaZOOnico"
  1. Tubing down the river
  1. Visit another local family, seeing their place, their way of living and using a blowgun
  1. Visit a cayman lake
  1. All locations visited are reaches by motorized canoe over the river
  1. Walking through the rain forrest [although we didn't really go to a wild deserted patch of forrest] is an experience by itself
Places to stay:
We stayed in the "La Casa Del Suizo" lodge which has a lot of luxury and is quite expensive. Beautiful views and a nice calm relaxed place, swimming pool.
I liked it but the things I could have enjoyed more were the following:
  • More experiences out in the "unmanaged" forrest. Now the places we visited always involved a local family. Now it really felt like the families were a touristic attraction, that just feels a bit weird and makes a touristic, privilledged bubble.
  • The lodge itself was quite closed from the surroundings, this made that you didn't completely feel like you are in the middle of the amazon
  • Maybe do a group tour, for now we were woth 2 and got a private guide. Group tours can help the price maybe?

Baños:
Good to know before:
  1. Baños is mostly geared towards outdoor "extreme" sports/experiences. Think of: ziplining, kayaking, rafting, bungy jumping. But you van make nice hikes too
Experiences [1 full day]
  1. Hike to "la Casa del Atbol.", through passing "Mirador El Virgen" going down through café "café Del Cielo" and past "Mirador El Virgen" again back to Baños. [don't do this after it had rained or when it's raining, the steep paths get quite tough and slippery] [neglect the straight lines going from Baños to the top, those were errors in my GPS location; https://www.komoot.nl/tour/1222492845?ref=avs&share_token=alkzviyenmXng3i88kP4HuHsPNOBRp2H2mK9bgMWFwYyrSWXvi]
  1. "Ruta de las cascadas" , you can do this by mountainbike. The route is amazing and quite a bit downhill [not steep]. Take the mountainbike back by bus from Puyo. [I've not done this but the route is amazing]
  1. El Pailon Del Diablo, go down both sides. It's will cost you a whole $2 for going down each sit de but both offer a very different view on the waterfalls and are both to be recommended.
  1. Although we didn't, the ziplining would be really cool too! From Baños to Puyo you go through a narrow, lush valley. You can zipline from one to the other side.
Places to stay:
  • "Hostal Transilvania", nothing really special, although the breakfast[for us included] was really nice. It has a "kitchen" and the people are really nice. It's quite a bit of a social place but not the easiest on meeting people.
"Must-know's" :
  1. Terminal Terrestre in the maps list
  1. "Aromi café" [see the masp list], nice coffee in Baños
  1. Bar with swings [all seats are swings and there are some games.] I didn't go here but it looks nice and funny
  1. Cafe del Cielo, looks amazing, sadly we couldnt visit with the time during the hike. [the café is located on the hill/mountain next to Baños, I don't know how accessible it is. See maps list]

Riobamba:
Goot to know before:
  1. Riobamba is a good place to stay if you want to[partially] climb Vulcan Chimborazo or other volcanoes in the neighborhood like El Altar, etc.
  1. Be prepared for the hight of the Chimborazo, take it easy when hiking and take all the time to not feel too bad.
Experiences: [1 full day]
  1. There is a "viewpoint" in the city. It is just a park that's elavated a bit but this does give a nice overview of Riobamba aswell as Vulcan Chimborazo. [see maps list]
  1. Reserva de producion de fauna Chimborazo. From the entrance of the park you can walk up to 2 separate "refugio's". You can take lift maybe to the first hut and from there hike 45min to the 2nd hut. Walking up and down from the entrance of the park would be roughly 5 hours. The first and main part would be a road that the cars follow up to the first hut. [There is an option of mountainbiking down from the 1st hut to the entrance of the park. You would have to book this in Riobamba probably. There is a cool track straight down the mountain for which a guide is needed, otherwise follow the road that the cars take up and down] Note: going down by mountainbike might be tough with altitude sickness so best to try and do some acclimatization before. While hiking down faster I got a headache which might have been from the hight. To finish this one: go early, taking a bus tu Guarana and ask the driver beforehand to get out at the entrance of the Reserva Chimborazo. For the way back you can stop any bus going towards Riobamba by waving next to the road at the entrance of the park. To ensure succes, it would be wise to go back before the park closes since the busses might be more frequent. The park closes at 15:00?
  1. I've heard of apossible  Inca trail onto Chimborazo, although I didn't look into this. This could be very nice for the very proactive hikers out there.
Places to stay:
  • I've heard good stories about the "Bonitas hostel"
  • The place I stayed is not to be recommended. We stayed in Puruwa Hostel which was not taken care of too much, was noisy, the room smelled a bit, but; had a party lamp haha.
"Must-know's"
  1. Go early to Chimborazo to have enough time and have good options for getting back to Riobmaba. So mind the time ans plan carefully ahead.
  1. Terminal Terreste Riobamba, from here you can take a lot of busses.
  1. Specie Cafe, a small but very nice place to have a very good coffee. This would go towards specialty coffees and is maybe a bit more expensive.

Latacunga:
Good to know before:
  1. Latacunga is easy to reach from many places. Busses go from Quito, Baños, Ambato, Riobamba and much more probably.
  1. Coming from Riobamba, we got dropped off at the highway by the bus and had to take a taxi to the hostel from there. Latacunga is not that big so this is not too bad.
Experiences: [1 full day, and some half ones]
  1. "Parque Nacional Cotopaxi; from our hostel there was a tour through the national park Cotopaxi. This included the drive there[1/2 hours], time to partially climb the volcano [onto the refugio and beyond if you want. Time is limited though], visit a laguna in the park, lunch and a drive back [total $40/person].
  1. In the maps list I saved some places that were nice or recommended by the Lonely Planet: Mercado Cerrado Latacunga, Parque Vincente León, Mirador El Calvario, some restaurants and a nice supermarket "Tía" in the northern part
Places to stay:
  • Hostal Rosita; maybe not in the best neighborhood bust a nice hostel with a good breakfast for $3 extra and the tour to Cotopaxi is organised from here. Its not really a social place.
"Must-know's":
  1. Latacunga is the place to go before starting the Quilotoa loop. From the busstation you find busses to starting points for the loop

Quilotoa:
Good to know before:
  1. The "Quilotoa loop" is not necessarily a hike going in a loop. The "loop" refers to the crater you can hike around I think. The 3 day route like we did it would be; 1. Sigchos - Isinlivi. 2. Isinlivi - Chugchilán. 3. Chugchilán - Quilotoa and from Quilotoa back to Latacunga, or continuing the trip to somewhere else.
  1. The route is mostly given by red and yellow marks along the paths and road.
  1. You can easily walk the first part from Sigchos to Isinlivi on the day you take the bus. For us it was about a 3,5h to 4h hike.
  1. Bring enough water and food for the hikes. Although you can get good lunch from some hostels where you stay, on the way you cannot really buy stuff.
  1. You can leave your big backpack in Latacunga and take a smaller one for hiking the loop. [there is a recommendation for this in the Lonely Planet, we asked at our hostel [see "Latacunga" above] where we could leave it]
Experiences: [2 full days]
  1. Sigchos - Isinlivi
  1. Isinlivi - Chugchilán
  1. There is a nice viewpoint on the way to Chugchilán.
  1. Chugchilán - Quilotoa
  1. Go down to the water in the Quilotoa crater
Places to stay:
  • The Llullu llama hostel in Isinlivi is very nice and offers good info aswell as services for the loop [like a lunchpacket] this is also a very social place but can be easy and rerelaxed if you want.
  • We stayed at the "Chukirawa" hostel in Quilotoa which was fine but not the best place. It had a nice relaxing area, small rooms and decent food.
"Must-know's" :
  1. Hiking the loop in 2 days is possible as we did it but I imagine it being way nicer to take 3 days. Combining day 2 and day 3 together makes for a really tough program.
  1. Busses from Quilotoa leave towards Latacunga. To get the bus you should go to the main road, just outside of the gate into Quilotoa [see maps list]
